IMPORTANT NOTE REGARDING ADVANCE RELEASES:

This dataset and all accompanying documentation are part of an "Advance
 Release" of CSES Module 3 (2006-2011).

By definition, an Advance Release is a preliminary version of a dataset,
and thus lacks some of the checking, cleaning, processing, documentation,
data, and variables that are usual to the Full Release of a dataset.  Many
election studies that will eventually be present in the CSES Module 3 Full 
Release are not available in this file. 

Advance Releases are provided as a service to the CSES user community,
for those analysts who find it valuable to work with preliminary versions
of the dataset.  We would appreciate being notified of any errors in the
dataset or documentation by email to "cses@umich.edu".  

Users should expect future changes and improvements to the naming, data,
and documentation of variables and election studies that appears in an 
Advance Release file.  If users wish to re-use their programming code on
a future release of the file, the code should be written in a way that is
flexible and can be accomodating of these future changes.

Users of the Advance Release may also wish to monitor the errata for
CSES Module 3 on the CSES website, to check for known errors which may 
impact their analyses.  To view errata for CSES Module 3, go to the Data
Center on the CSES website, navigate to the CSES Module 3 download page, 
and click on the Errata link in the grey box to the right of the page.

We hope that until such time as the Full Release of CSES Module 3 is
available, users will find this and future CSES Module 3 Advance Releases
to be helpful in their work.

         | NOTES: PARTIES AND LEADERS
         |
         | This appendix identifies parties active during a polity's
         | election and (where available) their leaders.
         |
         | The party labels are provided for the codes used in the 
         | micro data variables. Parties A through F are the six 
         | most popular parties, listed in descending order according 
         | to their share of the  popular vote in the "lowest" level 
         | election held (i.e., wherever possible, the first segment 
         | of the lower house).
         |
         | Leaders A through F are the corresponding party leaders or
         | presidential candidates referred to in the micro data items.
         | This appendix reports these names and party affiliations.
         |
         | Parties G, H, and I are supplemental parties and leaders
         | voluntarily provided by some election studies. However,
         | these are in no particular order.
         |
         | If parties are members of electoral blocs, the name of 
         | the bloc is given in parentheses following the appropriate 
         | party labels.

         | ELECTION STUDY NOTES - GERMANY (2009): PARTIES AND LEADERS
         |
         | Remarks on CSES coding scheme for PARTY_C and PARTY_D:
         | 
         | In general, party labels A to I are order similar to the vote
         | share at the lowest tier, referring to the first vote choice in
         | the German national election. Consequently, the Left Party
         | received 11.1%, which was greater than the FDP (9.4%). However,
         | due to the second vote choice and the final distribution of
         | seats, the FDP got 93 seats (14.6%), compared
         | to 76 seats (11.9%) of the Left Party. Due to the distribution
         | of the seats, the FDP was defined as PARTY_C and the Left as 
         | PARTY_D.
         |
         | Remarks on parties and party leaders:
         | 
         | The CDU is not eligible for election in Bavaria (election 
         | districts 213 to 257, region 9), while the CSU is eligible for
         | election in Bavaria exclusively.
         |
         | Variables C3010_H and C3012_H refer to Mr Karl-Theodor zu
         | Guttenberg, the German minister of defense, who is no official
         | leader of the CSU, but actually the most important minister and
         | most popular politician of this party.
         |
         | The actual leaders of the Green Party neither had been Renate
         | Künast nor Jürgen Trittin. Instead, the dual leadership was
         | kept by Claudia Roth and Cem Özdemir, who were not listed
         | in the original German questionnaire.
         |
         | The WASG was not eligible for election in 2009. Instead
         | the party fused in 2007 with the PDS and formed the Left Party.
         |
         | The actual leaders of the Left Party in 2009 had been Oskar
         | Lafontaine (included in C3010_D and C3012_D) and Lothar Bisky, 
         | and neither were listed in the questionnaire. Rather,
         | variables C3010_I and C3012_I refer to Gregor Gysi, who is the
         | most important politician of the Left Party beside Oskar
         | Lafontaine.
         |
         | C4004_G reports the district results for the National Democratic
         | Party (NPD), which was not included in the micro-questionnaire,
         | variables C3009_G, C3010_G, C3011_G, and C3012_G.

         | ELECTION STUDY NOTES - HONG KONG (2008): PARTIES AND LEADERS
         |
         | The electoral system of Hong Kong Legislative Council
         | distinguishes between geographical and functional 
         | constituencies. In both constituencies 30 LegCo-members are
         | elected. The functional constituencies are due to professionals
         | or economical and social interest groups. For the CSES data,
         | C3023_LH_DC and C3032_LH_DC refer to the vote choice of 
         | the geographical constituencies, in which all registered
         | citizens are eligible to vote. 
         |
         | Researchers interested in the individual vote choices within the
         | functional constituencies might contact our collaborator of Hong
         | Kong (see introduction part) to get access to the data of 
         | origin.
         |
         | Researchers should note that in contrast to
         | the given coding schema, referring to parties, the original 
         | answers were candidate mentions. Accordingly, C3023_LH_DC 
         | and C3032_LH_DC only includes candidates' parties that have been
         | registered for respondents' geographical constituencies. In
         | contrast, C3028_LH_ and C3030_LH_ also refer to candidates'
         | parties outside respondents' geographical constituencies.
         | Moreover, according to C3030_LH_, our collaborators noted 
         | that the label "'independents' in Hong Kong means 'not 
         | affiliated with any political parties' and does not necessarily
         | mean that the candidates' political inclinations are different
         | from those of major political parties".
         |
         | The Hong Kong Federation of Trade Unions (PARTY E) is not 
         | included in C3009_E, C3010_E and C3011_E, due to not being part
         | of the questionnaire of origin. However, information about the
         | party is available in the district, as well as in the macro part
         | of CSES. Moreover, the official leader of The Hong Kong 
         | Federation of Trade Unions in 2008 has been Cheng Yiu-Tong, who 
         | was not mentioned in C3008_2.
